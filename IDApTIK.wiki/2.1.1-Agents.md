This page discusses player characters in the game in terms of appearance, abilities, levelling, class, and so on and so forth.

# Appearance

Within the pixel art style, characters will be somewhat limited in customisation. Due to the multiplayer intention of this game, we suggest some level of customisation, whether it be a select few avatars or some sort of combinatorial approach plus maybe HSL sliders or set colour palettes. Preset characters will exist with whichever system is in place. .vcf of characters is a suggestion for modding and sharing.

# Attributes

A similar levelling system to that in _Shadowrun_ seemed like a good place to start given the discrete ability improvement we want to see with this game, with independent attribute XP (opposed to common pool) to encourage attempting different playstyles. # Background

Agents will start out with skill buffs that allow for greater-than-max abilities. For instance, the ability to knock down a class of doors that are impossible for even top-level doorkicker without this background to do. This causes top-level characters to be different from each other and gives direction when starting out.

# Class

Class may be defined based on skill level, as a note of what a character is best at (for example, a competent swordsman with high constitution be "Adept"), or selected for some kind of bonus instead of Background.

Broad ideas below for playstyles to help us look at the important aspects of gameplay to look at: <br />
Hacker - Computing systems infiltration <br />
Engineer - Electrical systems rewiring <br />
Ghost - Building infiltration <br />
Infiltrator - Social engineering focus <br />
Scout - Recon and gadgets <br />
Saboteur - Causing chaos <br />
Breacher - Frontal assault

# Multiplayer

Multiplayer will allow for cooperative gameplay, where two (maybe more) players will have the advantage of using their skills and playstyles to work together. These two players are expected to experience completely different gameplay (what I dub asymmetric cooperative), where a hacker would be playing _Uplink_ while a breacher would be playing _Doorkickers: Action Squad_...in principle. This is similar to different roles in _Barotrauma_, where proficiency in one area does not prevent attempt in any other area.

A similar suggestion is that of asymmetric PVP, with missions that may look as follows: <br />
Infiltrator vs Security <br />
Prisoner (escape) vs Hitman (vs Security?) <br />
Courier vs Interceptor <br />
Rescuer vs Kidnapper <br />
Heist vs Response

With decent enough AI, a cooperative team could work together against these groups, etc, even supposing we choose not to have players on each side of these missions (balancing would probably be very challenging).
