# Gameplay Mechanics & Loop

IDApTIK is built around **asymmetric cooperative gameplay**. Two players experience fundamentally different games that interlock into a single operation.

## The Core Dynamic

The **infiltrator** moves through the world—jumping, crawling, ducking, dodging obstacles, all while staying out of sight. But doors don't open themselves. Elevators don't wait forever. Security systems don't just turn off.

That's where the **hacker** comes in. But the hacker isn't some disembodied voice in a van—they exist in the game world too. Maybe they're in the same building, hunched over a terminal in a server closet. Maybe they're in a cafe across town, hoping the barista doesn't notice them running port scans on a corporate network. Maybe they're on another continent entirely, bouncing their connection through a dozen compromised machines.

The hacker controls access points, rewires electronics, and times disruptions with precision—or, at least, they should.

Because if the infiltrator jumps too soon, or the hacker mistimes an override, things spiral fast. This isn't just about skill. It's about trust.

And trust has consequences. And justice matters.

## Physical Presence Matters

Both players have physical stakes:

- The **infiltrator** risks discovery by guards, cameras, and environmental hazards
- The **hacker** risks discovery too—using a corporate terminal you're not supposed to touch, or hacking from a location that gets traced back to you

Environmental factors affect both roles:
- Cause a power blackout to disable security? Hope you're not in the affected area too.
- Network latency, signal strength, and power availability can impact hacking operations.
- Some targets simply require physical access—the infiltrator plugs in, the hacker does the rest.

## Fluid Roles

While players may specialize, roles aren't locked. A hacker *can* pick a lock—just badly. An infiltrator *can* attempt to hack a terminal—just slowly and noisily. Proficiency creates efficiency, not exclusivity.

This mirrors games like _Barotrauma_, where any crew member can attempt any task, but specialists excel at their domain.
